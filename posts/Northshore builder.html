[FAQ](#faq) --- ## What "Custom" Actually Means on the North Shore {#what-custom-means} "Custom home builder" covers two distinct types of business in the North Shore market. **Fully custom builders** work from your brief and your site. Every design decision — floor plan, structural approach, ceiling height, material specification — is made for your block. These builders typically work on 6–12 projects per year and employ or regularly engage an in-house architect or work alongside the architect you appoint. **Semi-custom builders** work from a library of base designs they modify. They adapt facades, move non-load-bearing walls, and upgrade specifications. These are legitimate businesses and deliver good outcomes on unconstrained sites — but the result is a modified project home, not a ground-up custom design. On the North Shore — particularly in Mosman, Cremorne, Neutral Bay, Kirribilli, and Pymble — steep sites, heritage overlays, and council DAs that require specialist consultants mean most serious builds cannot use a base-plan library. A semi-custom builder's portfolio of plans often simply won't work on the block. If a builder shows you a catalogue before asking about your site, you know which category they fall into. --- ## How to Vet a Builder Before You Meet Them {#vet-a-builder} ![Architect with hard hat reviewing plans at a modern home exterior](https://images.pexels.com/photos/7937745/pexels-photo-7937745.jpeg?auto=compress&cs=tinysrgb&dpr=2&h=650&w=940) *Photo by [Pavel Danilyuk](https://www.pexels.com/@pavel-danilyuk) via Pexels* Do this research before the first phone call, not after. **Check the NSW Fair Trading licence.** Any builder working on residential projects over $20,000 in NSW must hold a current contractor licence. Search the [NSW Fair Trading licence register](https://www.fairtrading.nsw.gov.au/trades-and-businesses/contractor-licences) using the builder's business or trading name. Verify it is current, not suspended, and matches the legal entity that will sign your contract. **Read the NCAT record.** The NSW Civil and Administrative Tribunal publishes decisions. A builder with multiple NCAT appearances for defect disputes or non-completion is a pattern, not bad luck. A single historic dispute handled promptly is different. **Visit a completed project — not a display.** Ask the builder for contact details of clients who moved in 3–4 years ago. Call those clients directly: what went wrong during the build, how did the builder respond, would they build with them again. Builders who refuse this request are telling you something. **Ask who manages your site.** On the North Shore, a principal with a strong reputation will often assign your build to a site manager you have not yet met. That manager's experience and communication style is what you are actually buying. Meet them before you sign. **Check subcontractor continuity.** A builder who uses the same carpenter, tiler, and electrician across projects over many years delivers more consistent quality than one who prices trades fresh on each job. Ask directly who they use and how long those relationships have existed. --- ## What North Shore Custom Builds Cost in 2026 {#costs} ![Aerial view of a luxury custom home with manicured gardens](https://images.pexels.com/photos/9150612/pexels-photo-9150612.jpeg?auto=compress&cs=tinysrgb&dpr=2&h=650&w=940) *Photo by [Erik Mclean](https://www.pexels.com/@erikmclean) via Pexels* North Shore custom home builds in 2026 sit across three broad tiers: | Tier | Construction cost per m² | What you get | |------|--------------------------|--------------| | Entry custom | $3,500–$4,200 | Custom layout, standard-grade fixtures, builder's preferred suppliers | | Mid custom | $4,200–$5,500 | Architectural finishes, engineered timber, full builder–architect coordination | | High-end custom | $5,500–$8,000+ | Full architectural intent realised, premium imported materials, bespoke joinery, complex structural features | These figures are construction only. They exclude: - Land purchase - Demolition and asbestos removal (common on older North Shore blocks) - Development Application and Construction Certificate fees - Architect, structural engineer, geotechnical engineer, and surveyor fees - Landscaping, pool, driveway, and fencing - Furnishings and styling On a typical 600–750m² North Shore block, those additional costs often add $250,000–$500,000 to the total. A well-specified custom home on the North Shore regularly reaches $3M–$4M all-in before you consider the land. The most common budget error is calculating construction cost per m² on a flat block and applying it to a steeply graded site. Split-level designs on challenging topography cost materially more per m² to build — the structural complexity is different. --- ## The Realistic Timeline {#timeline} ![New home under construction — framing stage](https://images.pexels.com/photos/37627682/pexels-photo-37627682.jpeg?auto=compress&cs=tinysrgb&dpr=2&h=650&w=940) *Photo by [D Goug](https://www.pexels.com/@d-goug) via Pexels* A full custom home build on the North Shore from initial brief to handover typically runs 24–36 months. | Phase | Typical duration | |-------|-----------------| | Concept design and brief development | 2–4 months | | Development Application lodgement and determination | 6–18 months | | Construction Certificate | 1–2 months | | Construction | 12–18 months | | Defects period and handover | 1–3 months | DA timeframes on the North Shore are longer than many other Sydney councils. Heritage conservation areas cover large parts of Mosman, Hunters Hill, and Lane Cove LGAs. Applications affecting heritage items or within conservation areas routinely require additional reports — heritage impact statements, arborist reports, acoustic reports — that extend the assessment period. If your project requires a planning proposal, rezoning, or SEPP variation, add 6–12 months. Budget for the longer end of every range. Builders who promise 18-month all-in timelines on heritage-affected North Shore sites are not accounting for DA risk. --- ## When a Custom Builder Is the Wrong Choice {#wrong-choice} A fully custom builder is not the right fit for every project. Be honest about whether this applies to you: - **Your budget is under $900,000 all-in for new construction.** At that budget a semi-custom or volume builder will deliver better value. Custom builders have overhead structures that price their minimum viable project above this level. - **Your site is flat, rectangular, and carries no heritage or planning complications.** A project home can deliver 75–80% of the outcome for 55–60% of the cost on an unconstrained site. - **You need to be in the house within 14 months.** The DA process alone on most North Shore LGAs makes that timeframe unrealistic. If your timeline is driven by a hard constraint — lease expiry, school enrolment — a custom build is likely the wrong vehicle. - **You cannot commit regular time to a 12–18 month design and specification process.** Custom builds require 30–50 hours of active client involvement across design, selections, and specification sign-off. If your schedule won't support it, decisions stall, which extends timelines and increases costs. There is no shame in those answers. The right builder for your project is the one matched to your constraints — not the most prestigious name on the North Shore. --- ## Questions to Ask Before Signing {#questions} Before signing any building contract: 1. How many projects are currently under construction, and how many are in your confirmed pipeline for the next 12 months? (More than 8 active projects from a small firm is a capacity risk for your site supervision.) 2. Who will be my primary contact during the build — you, a dedicated project manager, or an administrator? Can I meet that person now? 3. Can I have contact details for the last three clients you handed over to, including one from two or more years ago? 4. What is your process for the 13-week post-handover defects inspection, and what is the typical response time for rectification? 5. What contract type do you use — HIA, MBA, or your own? Under what circumstances do you use cost-plus versus fixed-price? 6. How are subcontractor payments managed? If a dispute arises mid-build, how are subbies protected? A builder who deflects, shortens, or avoids these questions is showing you how they will handle problems during construction. --- ## FAQ {#faq} **How do I find reputable custom home builders on the North Shore?** Start with the [Housing Industry Association](https://hia.com.au) and [Master Builders Association NSW](https://www.mbansw.asn.au) member directories, both of which require ongoing professional development for membership. Verify the contractor licence on the NSW Fair Trading register and check NCAT decisions for any defect or payment disputes. **Do I need my own architect, or does the builder provide one?** Some custom builders have in-house architects; others work with independent practices. If you have a complex brief, a heritage site, or a strongly articulated design vision, engaging your own architect — who works for you rather than the builder — gives you a stronger check on design intent and build quality throughout construction. **What is a cost-plus contract and should I use one?** A cost-plus contract means you pay the builder's actual costs plus an agreed margin, typically 15–20%. It provides more transparency but less cost certainty. On highly complex North Shore projects it can be the right structure. On standard custom builds, a fixed-price contract with carefully defined provisional sum allowances is the safer starting point — it puts the cost risk where it belongs. **How long does a DA take in North Shore LGAs?** Mosman targets 40 days for straightforward DAs, but heritage-affected properties regularly run 6–12 months. Ku-ring-gai and Lane Cove have similar patterns. Budget a minimum of 8 months from DA lodgement to determination, and treat anything faster as a bonus. **What is a realistic all-in budget for a custom home on the North Shore?** A mid-tier custom build in 2026 runs approximately $4,200–$5,500/m² for construction. A 400m² home at the midpoint costs roughly $1.94M to build. Add land, demolition, consultants, DA fees, landscaping, pool, and furnishings, and the all-in number for a well-specified custom home on the North Shore regularly sits between $3M and $4.5M — excluding the cost of the land itself. --- *For more on choosing the right builder, see our guides on [how to choose a builder for a renovation](/blog/how-to-choose-a-builder-for-renovation), [knockdown rebuild builders in Sydney](/blog/knockdown-rebuild-builders-sydney), and [boutique home builders](/blog/boutique-home-builders).* *External references: [NSW Fair Trading Licence Register](https://www.fairtrading.nsw.gov.au/trades-and-businesses/contractor-licences) · [NCAT Decisions](https://www.ncat.nsw.gov.au) · [NSW Home Building Act 1989](https://legislation.nsw.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/act-1989-147)*
